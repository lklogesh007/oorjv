For Consumers

Real food, checked scientifically before it gets the OORJV mark. Here's what that means for you, and how to check it yourself.

Food as medicine

Why OORJV matters for you

Calories and nutrition labels only tell part of the story. Two bottles of "cold-pressed oil" can look identical and still be very different once you test what's actually inside — how oxidised it is, whether it's been cut with a cheaper oil, whether it still has the protective compounds it started with.

OORJV testing looks past the label. We check for adulteration, for oxidation damage (measured as MDA), and for the beneficial compounds — polyphenols and antioxidants — that make a food genuinely good for your family, not just "not obviously bad".

In short: a product only earns the OORJV mark after its actual contents are tested at AMMAGENOMICS Food Testing Lab — not because a company says it's natural, organic, or healthy on the packaging.

Reading the mark

What the OORJV label means

No adulteration

Tested to confirm nothing cheaper has been mixed in to fake volume or weight.

Safe to consume

Screened for pesticide residues, banned chemicals and additives beyond acceptable levels.

Healthy markers

Carries measurable levels of polyphenols and antioxidants — often higher than standard organic products.

Traceable batch

Every certificate is tied to a specific batch and verification number, not a blanket brand claim.
