What OORJV checks

Four markers decide whether a product earns the OORJV mark. Here's what each one means, in plain terms.

No adulteration

The product is checked for cheap fillers, mixed-in oils, sugar syrups, and other substances used to fake bulk or weight.

No harmful chemicals

We screen for pesticide residues, banned colouring agents, preservatives and other additives that shouldn't be in your food.

Low MDA

MDA (malondialdehyde) is a marker of oxidation damage. Lower MDA means the oil or fat hasn't broken down into harmful compounds.

High polyphenols & antioxidants

Naturally occurring compounds linked to better health outcomes — the markers most adulterated products lose first.
